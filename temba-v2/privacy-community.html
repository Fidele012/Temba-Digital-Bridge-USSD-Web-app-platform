Privacy Policy

For Community Members — Water Users & Residents of Rwanda

Effective Date: 16 July 2026  |  Rwanda Law No. 058/2021 on Personal Data Protection

This Privacy Policy explains exactly what personal information Temba Digital Bridge collects from community members, why we collect it, who can see it, how long we keep it, and what rights you have over it under Rwandan law. We are committed to handling your data responsibly and transparently.

1Who Controls Your Data

Temba Digital Bridge is the data controller for all personal information you provide when registering or using the platform as a community member. We determine how and why your data is processed. Contact us at tembadigitalbridge@gmail.com for any privacy-related matter.

3Why We Use Your Data

Every piece of data we collect serves a specific purpose directly related to helping you access water services:

  • Name & contact details — to create and identify your account, and so service providers can reach you about your reports, appointments, and requests.
  • Location data (province to village) — to route your report or request to the correct water service provider for your area and to include your location in the issue report.
  • Gender — to ensure gender-inclusive service delivery analysis and improve how providers respond to different members of the community.
  • Issue reports, appointments & service requests — to create, track, and resolve cases, and to provide you with status updates.
  • Ratings — to calculate anonymous aggregate scores that improve provider accountability. Your name is never linked to a rating.
  • Notification preferences — to send you updates only through the channels you have chosen.
  • Usage data — to maintain platform security, prevent fraud, and improve the platform's reliability.

4Who Can See Your Information

We do not sell your personal data. Access is limited to the following parties:

  • Water service providers — When you submit a report, appointment, or service request, the assigned provider sees your name, phone number, location, and the details of your case. They need this to contact you and resolve your issue. They do not see your password, PIN, or notification preferences.
  • Temba Digital Bridge team — Our administrators can access your account information for support, moderation, and platform maintenance purposes.
  • Government regulators — If legally required by Rwandan authorities (e.g. RURA, RIB), we may share data in response to a lawful order.
  • Technical service providers — Our hosting (Railway), email (Gmail SMTP), and SMS (Africa's Talking) providers process data on our behalf under confidentiality obligations.

Ratings you leave are anonymous. Providers see only the score and comment — never your name, email, or phone number.

5Your Location Data

Your location (province, district, sector, cell, and village) is the most operationally important piece of information on the platform. It determines:

  • Which water service provider is assigned to your report.
  • Where a technician will be dispatched for an appointment or service request.
  • How water quality data and service coverage maps are displayed.

We collect your location through the registration form — we do not track your real-time GPS position at any point. If you use USSD, your location is derived from what you enter during the session, not from your phone's GPS.

6Notifications & Communication

We will contact you through the channels you selected during registration:

  • Email — confirmation when you submit a case, and updates when a provider responds or resolves it.
  • SMS — the same events, delivered to your registered phone number via Africa's Talking.
  • In-app alerts — notifications in your dashboard when you are signed in.

You can change or disable any of these channels from your Profile page at any time. We will never send unsolicited marketing messages. Platform-critical messages (such as security alerts) may be sent even if you have disabled notifications.

8Your Rights Under Rwandan Law

Under Rwanda Law No. 058/2021 on Personal Data Protection and Privacy, you have the following rights as a community member:

  • Right to access — You can request a copy of all personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification — You can correct inaccurate information directly in your profile, or ask us to correct data we hold that you cannot update yourself.
  • Right to erasure — You can request that your account and personal data be permanently deleted. We will action this within 30 days, subject to any legal retention requirements.
  • Right to data portability — You can request your data in a machine-readable format (JSON or CSV).
  • Right to restrict processing — You can ask us to pause how we use your data while a complaint or query is being resolved.
  • Right to object — You can object to specific processing activities that you believe are not necessary for the service.
  • Right to complain — If you believe we have handled your data unlawfully, you may lodge a complaint with the Rwanda Data Protection Authority (RDPA).

To exercise any of these rights, email us at tembadigitalbridge@gmail.com with the subject line "Privacy Request — [your full name]". We will respond within 15 working days.

9Data Security

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data:

  • Passwords and USSD PINs are stored as secure hashes — we cannot read them.
  • All data transmitted between your device and our servers uses HTTPS encryption.
  • Access to the database is restricted to authorised platform administrators only.
  • We do not store payment card numbers or financial details of any kind.

No system is completely immune to security incidents. In the event of a data breach that affects your personal information, we will notify you by email within 72 hours of becoming aware of it, in line with Rwandan data protection requirements.

10Children's Privacy

Temba Digital Bridge is intended for users aged 16 and above. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 16. If a parent or guardian registers on behalf of a child, the adult's information is used for the account. If we become aware that data from a child under 16 was collected without appropriate consent, we will delete it promptly.

11Cookies & Local Storage

We do not use advertising or tracking cookies. The Temba web platform stores the following data locally in your browser:

  • Your session token — to keep you signed in across pages without requiring you to log in again each time.
  • Your notification history — so alerts remain visible in your dashboard between visits.
  • Your language preference — to remember whether you use English or Kinyarwanda.
  • Offline fallback data — a cached copy of your reports and appointments so the dashboard loads instantly even on slow connections.

This data is stored only on your own device and is never sent to third-party advertisers.

12Changes to This Policy

We will update this Privacy Policy when our data practices change or when Rwandan regulations require it. You will be notified of any material change via email (if enabled) and through an in-app announcement. The effective date at the top of this page will be updated accordingly.

Continued use of Temba Digital Bridge after a policy update means you accept the revised Privacy Policy.
