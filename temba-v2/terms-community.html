Terms of Use

For Community Members — Water Users & Residents of Rwanda

Effective Date: 16 July 2026  |  Governed by Rwandan Law

These Terms apply to you as a community member — a resident, household, or individual in Rwanda using Temba Digital Bridge to report water problems, book service appointments, or request water-related services. Please read them carefully before creating your account.

1Who We Are

Temba Digital Bridge is a digital platform built to bridge the gap between Rwandan residents and water service providers. We give community members a simple, accessible way to report water issues, track resolutions, and communicate with the providers responsible for their water supply — whether through our web platform or by dialling a USSD code on any basic mobile phone.

We are a facilitator. We do not supply water ourselves; we connect you with the providers who do.

2What You Can Do as a Community Member

By registering as a community member you can:

  • Report water problems — contamination, pipe bursts, no water supply, low pressure, billing disputes, and other issues — and track them to resolution.
  • Book appointments with a water service provider for inspections, meter installations, or technical visits.
  • Request services such as new connections, pipe repairs, or infrastructure checks.
  • Verify resolutions by confirming whether a reported issue has truly been fixed before it is closed.
  • Rate service quality anonymously after a case is resolved, helping improve accountability.
  • Receive notifications by SMS, email, and in-app alert when a provider responds to or updates your case.
  • Use USSD access by dialling the platform code on any basic phone — no internet or smartphone required.

3Creating Your Account

To use the platform you must create a community member account. You agree that:

  • All information you provide during registration — your name, phone number, email address, location (province to village), and gender — is accurate and truthful.
  • You will keep your login credentials (email + password, or phone + USSD PIN) secure and not share them with others.
  • Each person must create their own individual account. Sharing one account across multiple people is not permitted.
  • You are at least 16 years old. If you are under 16, a parent or guardian must register on your behalf.
  • You will notify us if you believe your account has been accessed by someone else.

Your account is personal to you. You may update your profile information, notification preferences, and password at any time from your dashboard.

4Reporting Water Issues

When you submit a report, you agree that:

  • The issue you describe is real and occurring in your community or household.
  • You will provide your accurate location (province, district, sector, cell, and village) so the right provider is notified.
  • You will not submit false, duplicate, or malicious reports designed to harm a service provider's reputation.
  • You understand that Temba forwards your report to the assigned water service provider — we do not resolve issues ourselves.
  • Response and resolution times depend on the provider's SLA commitments. Temba's escalation system automatically alerts supervisors and authorities when deadlines are missed.

You have the right to verify whether your issue has been resolved. Until you confirm resolution, the case remains open. If a provider marks an issue resolved without your agreement, you may dispute it through the platform.

5Booking Appointments

When you book an appointment:

  • Choose a date and time that you will genuinely be available for. Repeated no-shows may result in your appointment booking access being restricted.
  • If you need to cancel or reschedule, do so through the platform in advance so the provider can reallocate the slot to another community member.
  • The provider may propose an alternative time if your requested slot is unavailable. You will be notified and can accept, reject, or suggest another time.
  • Appointments are subject to the provider's working hours and availability. Temba is not responsible if a provider cancels due to operational constraints.

6Requesting Services

Service requests (such as new connections, infrastructure repairs, or meter installations) are directed to the water service provider for your area. By submitting a request:

  • You confirm that the service you are requesting relates to a genuine water supply or sanitation need.
  • You understand that approval, scheduling, and delivery of the service is the provider's responsibility.
  • You agree to provide accurate contact information so the provider can reach you to coordinate the service.
  • Any fees charged for services are set by and payable directly to the water service provider — not to Temba Digital Bridge.

7Notifications

During registration you choose how you want to be notified:

  • In-app alerts — notifications appear in your Temba dashboard when you are signed in.
  • Email notifications — sent to the email address you registered with when you submit or when a provider responds.
  • SMS notifications — sent to your registered phone number for the same events.

You can change your notification preferences at any time from your profile settings. Disabling a channel will stop notifications on that channel but will not affect the others. Critical system messages (such as account security alerts) may still be sent regardless of your preferences.

8USSD Access

Temba Digital Bridge is accessible via USSD for community members who do not have a smartphone or internet connection. When using USSD:

  • Your account is identified by your phone number and a 4-digit PIN.
  • You can report issues, track your cases, and book appointments without internet access.
  • USSD sessions are limited in length by your mobile network provider — if a session times out, you can dial in again to continue.
  • Standard mobile network charges (airtime) may apply for dialling USSD codes, depending on your operator and tariff.

9Your Rights as a Community Member

Temba Digital Bridge respects your rights. You have the right to:

  • Accurate information — to know the status of your reported issue at any time through your dashboard or USSD.
  • Dispute resolution — to challenge a provider's resolution claim if the issue has not truly been fixed.
  • Anonymous ratings — to rate a provider's service quality after resolution without your name being revealed to the provider.
  • Data access and deletion — to request a copy of your personal data or ask for your account to be deleted. See our Privacy Policy for details.
  • Language access — to use the platform in English or Kinyarwanda.
  • Equal treatment — to receive the same quality of service regardless of your location, gender, or economic status.

10Acceptable Use

You must not:

  • Submit false, fabricated, or deliberately misleading reports or service requests.
  • Use the platform to harass, threaten, or defame water service providers or their staff.
  • Attempt to access another community member's account or data.
  • Use automated tools or bots to submit reports or requests.
  • Share your account credentials with others or create accounts on behalf of people without their knowledge.
  • Use the platform for any purpose other than water and sanitation issue management.

Violations may result in your account being suspended or permanently deactivated.

11What Temba Does Not Guarantee

While we work hard to make this platform reliable, we want you to understand:

  • Temba does not guarantee that a water service provider will respond within any particular timeframe — our escalation system holds providers accountable, but we cannot force them to act.
  • Platform availability may be interrupted for maintenance, upgrades, or factors beyond our control.
  • USSD access depends on your mobile network's availability and is outside our control.
  • We are not responsible for the quality, accuracy, or outcome of services delivered by water service providers.
